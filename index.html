Friday, August 15th: It all starts with a Barbecue "Rehearsal Dinner" at Trillium Lake in the Mt. Hood National Forest.


We'll start cooking in the afternoon (around 2:00 PM or later), eating, drinking, playing, swimming - making merriment, in short - until we are ready to sleep. Feel free to camp right at Trillium Lake (campsites will be reserved), or book a nearby lodge (there are many in Government Camp).

We will set up camp on Thursday, August 14th, so any early arrivers can easily make camp the night before. The more campers, the merrier!

If you need a ride out to Trillium Lake from Portland, let us know!

12:00 PM, Saturday, August 16th: A small, lakeside wedding ritual at Trillium Lake Amphitheater.

The amphitheater is in the same general area as the picnic area, and will be in the backyard of those of you camping. For anyone staying in a lodge at Government camp, you will have about a 10 minute drive to the ceremony location.

There is no parking lot at the amphitheater, and each car must pay an entrance fee. You can park at the boat-launch area for a very short hike to the amphitheater, or arrange a ride into the campground from Government Camp or Timberline Lodge (Carpools! Yay!).

7:00 PM, Saturday, August 16th: Party at the Village Ballroom, 700 NE Dekum St, Portland, OR 97211

The reception will take place at the gorgeous Village Ballroom in Portland, OR. This will be about an hour drive from Trillium Lake, but you should have plenty of time to pack up camp, check-out of your hotels, and have a pleasant afternoon on Mt. Hood or in Portland.

Anyone who would like to help us set-up for the party at 6:00 PM, let us know. In the tradition of The Village Ballroom, we will all help clean at the end of the night (midnight), stacking up chairs, sweeping, and putting the place back in order.

We will have live music, our favorite microbrews, and a big spread of home-made dinner, thanks to our most excellent friends.

The Village Ballroom is in Northeast (NE) Portland, and is close to a number of hotels, inns, b&bs - not to mention Portland's very active Air BnB scene.

Thank you to all of our family and friends for sharing this special experience!

We are in the beginning stages of some very major changes in our lives, and we are unsure of what, exactly, the future will hold. While we await news from Graduate Schools, and weigh our many options for setting down roots, the love we share is perhaps the only constant we can plan on.

Given our situation, we would prefer you not shower us with wedding gifts. Until we set roots, and begin our household, it will be impossible for us to house the typical dowry (Oh how we yearn for 5 gallon crocks, Kitchen-Aid countertop mixers, and all of those lovely household staples, but we have no place to keep them).

While your presence is more than enough to fill both of our hearts to the brim with joy, any monetary gifts will be graciously accepted, and will assist us in our next phase of life, whether that be a down-payment on a house, or graduate school tuition.

So again, Thank you! We love you!
